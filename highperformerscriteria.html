• 2019 Photo Gallery Nomination Criteria

To acknowledge and recognize noteworthy contributions that support the department and Memorial’s overall objectives, mission, and goals.

  • Nominated employees must be in good standing, having received no counseling notices in the previous 12 months.
  • Nominees must have earned an “Exceeds” or “Outstanding” rating on their most recent annual evaluation.
  • Previous honorees, supervisors, managers, and department leaders are not eligible.
  • Nomination forms will be accepted approximately 60 days prior to the recognition event.
  • Human Resources will provide the nomination form and submission deadline to department leaders.
  • Nominations must include specific examples of actions that go above and beyond expectations and make a positive and recognizable difference to tdepartment or organization.
  • High Performers will be recognized during a luncheon coordinated by Human Resources.

Performance and behaviors are aligned with Memorial’s “pillars,” goals, objectives, and mission. See examples below.

Category Example of Nominee's Behavior and Work Performance
Service The nominee has demonstrated high levels of services excellence. She takes actions to ensure customers’ needs are fully met, and creates a positive impression and experience (e.g. she has improved efficiency and quality of customer service). She is recognized by colleagues as consistently exceeding Memorial’s standards of behavior.
Quality of work & innovation The nominee demonstrates exceptional quality, recommends quality improvement initiatives, identifies innovative ways to save resources and reduce costs, identifies existing problems and opportunities while developing effective solutions, and suggests ideas to improve work processes.
Teamwork & leadership The nominee demonstrates excellence in her ability to support coworkers and flexibility in accomplishing department goals. She inspires and mentors colleagues to excel, providing guidance and encouragement. She also consistently fosters and environment of collaboration and cooperation.
Safety The nominee promotes a culture of safety, identifying ways to improve processes and protocols that have positive impact on patient safety. She identifies situations that warrant immediate intervention.
Community service The nominee developed a plan that positively impacted the community. She demonstrates exceptional involvement in the community.
