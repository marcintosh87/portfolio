Visitor Policy

Memorial Healthcare System hospitals:

Visitors are not allowed, unless there is a special circumstance, starting Wednesday, March 18, at all Memorial hospital locations. There are two exceptions. Delivering mothers are allowed to have their spouse or significant other present and pediatric patients are allowed one parent or guardian.

Exceptions:

If you are asymptomatic, you are cleared to return to work. You must:

  • Joe DiMaggio Children’s Hospital patients are allowed two visitors (caregivers/guardian). There are no restrictions on visiting hours.
  • Maternity patients and patients in the emergency department are allowed one visitor 24/7.
  • Any other exceptions will be made by shift clinical leadership.

Joe DiMaggio Children’s Hospital

  • Only two visitors (caregivers/guardian) per patient 24/7 are allowed
  • Shift clinical leadership will determine if any exceptions are to be made

All visitors will be screened.

Every visitor will be screened upon entering our facilities. They may be asked questions like:

  • Have you had a fever in the last 3 days?
  • Do you have a runny nose?
  • Do you have a fever or flu-like symptoms and other signs of illness?
  • Have you had exposure to a cold and flu or travel to the affected COVID-19 areas?

Valet

  • Valet services are suspended at all Memorial hospitals except at the Medical Office buildings for each location.

Memorial Hospitals main entrances and emergency departments:

Valet at all Memorial Hospitals will be unavailable, with one valet person helping to direct traffic

Joe DiMaggio Children’s Hospital

Valet will remain open in the main entrance and emergency department

Elective Surgeries

Elective surgeries are currently restricted at all Memorial hospitals. Patients should call their physician’s office to find out if their surgery has been delayed.
