Travel

Memorial Healthcare System strongly urges you to avoid non-essential travel.

If you are returning from Level 2 travel or a cruise

If you are asymptomatic, you are cleared to return to work. You must:

  • Wear a surgical mask at work for 14 calendar days from the date of your return
  • Take your temperature twice a day for 14 calendar days from the date of your return and maintain a log
  • Remain alert for respiratory symptoms consistent with COVID-19(cough, shortness of breath, sore throat)
  • Be prepared to provide proof of travel

If you have or develop symptoms, you must:

  • Self-isolate and inform your supervisor and call your facility’s Employee Health office immediately
  • Do not report to Employee Health office without an appointment
  • Provide proof of travel and stay home for 14 calendar days
  • Take your temperature twice a day and monitor symptoms for 14calendar days and maintain a log
  • Schedule an appointment with your facility’s Employee Health office for return-to-work clearance (and bring your log)
  • Call your primary care physician and/or your nearest Emergency Department if you need medical attention and tell them your travel history before you visit

If you are returning from Level 3 countries with widespread, sustained community transmission (China, South Korea, Iran, Europe, United Kingdom. See CDC for complete list.)

  • Inform your supervisor and call your facility’s Employee Health office immediately
  • Do not report to Employee Health office without an appointment
  • Self-isolate and stay home for 14 calendar days
  • Take your temperature twice a day and monitor symptoms for 14calendar days and maintain a log
  • Schedule an appointment with your facility’s Employee Health office for return-to-work clearance (and bring your log)
  • Provide proof of travel
  • Call your primary care physician and/or your nearest Emergency Department if you need medical attention and tell them of your travel history before you visit
  • Critically needed health care workers who are asymptomatic may return to work following approval byCOVID-19 Clinical Steering Committee. These individuals should still report temperatures and absence of symptoms each day prior to starting work and wear a surgical mask while at work for 14 days after the exposure event.

Any Exposure to a Known Covid-19 Patient

  • Inform your supervisor and call your facility’s Employee Health office immediately
  • Do not report to Employee Health office without an appointment
  • Provide proof of exposure to COVID-19 positive patient
  • Take your temperature twice a day and monitor symptoms for 14calendar days and maintain a log
  • Schedule an appointment with your facility’s Employee Health office for return-to-work clearance (and bring your log)
  • Call your primary care physician and/or your nearest Emergency Department if you need medical attention and tell them of your travel history before you visit
  • Critically needed health care workers who are asymptomatic may return to work following approval byCOVID-19 Clinical Steering Committee. These individuals should still report temperatures and absence of symptoms each day prior to starting work and wear a surgical mask while at work for 14 days after the exposure event.
  • Recommendations for work restrictions based on CDC guidance in accordance with expert consensus by Employee Health and Infectious Disease.

How you will be paid if you have to stay home for 14 calendar days

  • If the activity keeping you home was not Memorial-sponsored, the first 24 hours of your pay will be paid from your pleave balance and the remaining hours will be paid from your disability leave
  • If you were asked by Memorial to stay home for an event that was Memorial-sponsored or happened while you were at woyou will be paid from your disability leave from day one
