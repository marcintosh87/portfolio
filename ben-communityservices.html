Community Services

Memorial Healthcare System is well-known for the programs it offers our wider community. Memorial’s Community Services initiatives address and connect Broward County residents to social supports such as educational and benefits programs, family planning, affordable child care, jobs and financial assistance, and high-quality, preventive healthcare. All help provides the foundation for lifelong health and well-being, and contribute to the health and vibrancy of our community.

As a Memorial employee, you can take advantage of Community Services programs, too! All services are accredited by the Council of Accreditation for Rehabilitation Services (CARF) and licensed by the Department of Children and Families for demonstrating a commitment to Standards of Excellence. We are an approved provider with Broward County Public Schools to deliver behavioral health services.

Family Ties

(Theraputic Intervention to Empower and Strengthen)

An In-Home Family Prevention Model Program

Family TIES works to prevent substance abuse, preserving the family and promoting academic achievement.

How it works – A representative will come to your home to provide a family-centered approach for developing effective parenting skills and strengthening your family. The goal is to create opportunities to promote quality time and family bonding. Your family will:

  • Set goals for success
  • Practice effective family communication skills
  • Develop strategies for doing well in school
  • Have clear expectations about drugs and alcohol use
  • Be set up to prevent relapses

Healthy Youth Transitions (HYT)

Addressing the Transition to Independent Living

HYT promotes the skills that young people need to transition into living independently and productively.

HHow it works – If you have a young person in your household who is about to transition out of foster care, HYT can help with strategies to develop the life, academic and employability skills needed to move into healthy young adulthood. HYT targets youth in the foster system and in relative and non-relative caregiver living arrangements.

Your family will receive:

  • Counseling
  • Case management
  • Job coaching
  • Connections to resources in the community
  • Hands-on activities to address social, emotional and behavioral needs

Healthy Start

A Care Coordination and Home Visiting Model for At-Risk Moms

‍Healthy Start’s focus is on at-risk prenatal and postnatal women.

How it works – If you’re a new mom or mom-to be who is at risk for depression and other mental health challenges, Healthy Start will visit your home to help you learn how to care for your baby – and for yourself.

You can access:

  • Childbirth education
  • Breastfeeding assistance
  • Parenting education
  • Care coordination
  • Nutritional education
  • Midwifery
  • Links to WIC and Medicaid
  • Teen pregnancy program (at Henry D. Perry Education Canter, Miramar)

KISS

(Kinship Initiative For Supportive Services)

‍‍KISS works to strengthen families and reduce risk factors for children in relative or non-relative kinship care.

How it works – If you’re coping with unexpected family caregiving responsibilities, KISS provides comprehensive and coordinated services, both in your home and at convenient community locations.

You can receive help with:

  • Case management
  • Parent and family skills training
  • Legal advocacy

Memorial Cares

(Caregivers Access To Resources, Education And Support)

If you’re one of the 40% of American adults who is a caregiver, you’re dealing with all the extra responsibilities that caring for an adult or a child with significant health issues can bring. Having a job in addition to your caregiver role means that you can often feel overwhelmed, disconnected and lonely. And life can become even more daunting with the physical demands and emotional stress that caregiving involves.

That’s where Memorial CARES (Caregivers Access to Resources, Education and Support) comes in. Formerly known as Leeza’s Care Connection, Memorial CARES provides you with the guidance and support you need if you’re caring for a loved one with a chronic illness.

Resources include:

  • Better Breathers – A program of the American Lung Association
  • Caregivers Support Groups – To manage daily living, cope with stress and bereavement, and access community resources
  • Caregivers Support Groups – To manage daily living, cope with stress and bereavement, and access community resources
  • Alzheimer’s/Dementia Support Group – Facilitated by the Alzheimer’s Association
  • Stress Reduction/Exercise Groups
  • Weekly Speakers and Presentations
  • Social Activities

Memorial Senior Partners

Allies (Adults Living Life Independently, Educated And Safe)

‍‍Do you have a family member who is over the age of 85 and living alone? Older adults can face issues of social isolation, lack of independence and loneliness – all of which can contribute to their mental and physical decline. ALLIES addresses these issues by helping your older loved one living at home maintain his or her health and independence. The program uses evidence-based practices that promote prevention, health improvement and self-care.

ALLIES provides:

  • In-home safety assessments
  • Help in obtaining local resources
  • Preventive education, including educational sessions and safety supports

Services from ALLIES are available at no cost. Contact (954) 326-0920 for more information.
